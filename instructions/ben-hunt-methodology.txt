📈 3. ANALYZING THE CURRENT LANDSCAPE
Stage 1: Determining awareness stages
The classic Hunt's Ladder has 5 levels:
Level 0: No problem (unaware of the need)
Level 1: Unaware problem (feeling discomfort)
Level 2: Searching for solutions (recognized the problem)
Level 3: Choosing a method (comparing approaches)
Level 4: Comparing products (choosing a provider)
Level 5: Ready to buy (making a decision)
Stage 2: Audience distribution
Based on data, determine:
What percentage of the audience is at each level
Main characteristics of each group
Speed of transition between levels
Barriers and triggers for movement
Stage 3: Building the decision-making path
For each transition between levels, describe:
What insights the person should receive
What objections to overcome
What evidence to see
What emotions to experience

⚠️ IMPORTANT APPLICATION PRINCIPLES
1. Individuality of each product:
Hunt's Ladder is a tool that needs to be adapted for a specific product, audience, and market. Universal solutions do not exist.
2. Dynamic process:
The audience can move between levels in both directions. It's important to constantly track and adjust the strategy.
3. Data quality:
The effectiveness of the analysis directly depends on the quality of the source data. It's better to conduct fewer interviews, but high-quality ones.
4. Focus on results:
The goal is not to create a beautiful analysis, but to achieve concrete sales results.
5. Testing and adjustment:
The strategy must be constantly tested and adjusted based on real feedback from the audience.
