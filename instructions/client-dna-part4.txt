Part #4 Life After Solving the Problem: Transformation, Fears, and Market Expectations
Magical Transformation
If a magic genie could snap their fingers and give them the most ideal solution, what would this transformation and subsequent life of my clients look like?
How would they feel immediately after the transformation? What emotions would they experience?
What would change in their self-perception, self-confidence, attitude toward life?
How would their daily habits, thinking, decisions change?
What new opportunities would open up for them?
How would they talk about their problem in the past? What phrases would they use remembering it?
What new identity would they acquire after the transformation?
How would this transformation affect different key relationships in my clients' lives?
How would their relationship with loved ones change (partner, family, children, parents)?
How would they now feel among friends, colleagues, community?
How would society start perceiving them differently?
What complexes and barriers would leave their lives?
How would their relationship with money, work, business change?

Market Specifics
What does the market need to see to consider a product successful?
What key results (tangible and emotional) does the market expect from the product?
How does the market define "success"? What is its indicator that the product actually works?
What trust signals are important for the market to recognize a product as effective?
What social proof (case studies, reviews, success stories) is most convincing?
What brands, ideas, values does the market perceive as authoritative and reliable?
What does the market lose by refusing to address its problem?
What money, opportunities, time, health, energy does the client lose by postponing the problem's solution?
How does the absence of transformation destroy their prospects, dreams, goals?
What is the cost of delay if the client doesn't solve the problem now?
How does refusing to solve the problem affect their status, position, reputation, self-esteem?
What do they miss forever if they don't act right now?
Who does the market blame for its problem?
Do they blame themselves (self-criticism, guilt, low self-esteem)?
Or do they shift blame to others (government, employer, partner, competitors)?
Do external circumstances they consider insurmountable affect the problem?
Are there competing opinions and misconceptions preventing the market from seeing the solution?
How does a sense of injustice make them fight, resist, look for someone to blame?
Top 5 Biggest Market Objections
"It's too expensive, I can't afford it."
"I've already tried something similar, and it didn't help me."
"I'm not sure this is exactly what I need."
"I'm afraid it will take too much time and effort."
"What if I spend the money and don't get results?"
