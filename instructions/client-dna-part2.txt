Part #2 "Pains, Fears, and Triggers: What Pushes the Client Toward a Decision?"
Main Problems
3-5 main, key problems that my potential clients face (which leads them to consider buying my or a competing product)
What trigger events make them realize this problem?
How long did they ignore it before understanding that they can't do without a solution?
What attempts to solve the problem have they already made but didn't get results?
Why don't they believe the problem is solvable, and what myths do they have about it?
What exactly creates the unbearability of this problem and makes them seek a solution?
Top 5 Most Powerful Emotions Around This Problem
What primary emotion does the client experience every day because of this problem?
How exactly does it affect their self-esteem, confidence, internal state?
What emotion do they fear most if the problem isn't solved?
What emotions do they try to hide from others, but these emotions are eating them up inside?
What emotion do they want to feel instead (what would be liberation for them)?
Top 5 Biggest Fears About How the Problem Can Worsen and Deteriorate Life, and the Consequences It Will Lead To
What does the worst-case scenario look like if they do nothing?
How do they justify their inaction, but deep down understand they're sliding into an abyss?
What will happen to them in 1 year, 3 years, 5 years if they don't find a solution?
How does this fear tear them apart inside, but they're afraid to admit it even to themselves?
How does this fear make them procrastinate and postpone the solution, yet they suffer even more?
5 Ways These Fears Affect Key Relationships and Areas of the Client's Life
How does this problem destroy their relationships with loved ones, partner, friends, colleagues?
How does it make them feel worse among others (social discomfort, feeling of inadequacy)?
How does it affect their work, business, income, preventing them from growing and developing?
How does it destroy their physical and emotional health (stress, insomnia, psychosomatics)?
How does it take away their joy of life, forcing them to live in anxiety, fear, shame?
5 Conversational but Very Painful Phrases That My Clients Might Hear from Other People Regarding These Problems
"You're at it again? When are you going to figure this out already?"
"See, others managed it, but you didn't..."
"Who even cares about your problem? It's all in your head!"
"You always say you'll fix this, but nothing changes."
"I think you've just accepted it and don't want to change anything..."
Pain Situations
3-5 or more key pain situations that the client finds themselves in before realizing the problem is acute, which triggers the desire to solve it immediately
They receive a harsh trigger signal (conversation, situation, news) that shatters their illusions and forces them to acknowledge the problem.
They suffer another defeat that they can no longer ignore (something broke, failed, lost, couldn't do).
They compare themselves to someone who dealt with this problem, and it causes acute pain and a sense of their own incompetence.
Their problem is publicly exposed in front of others, they experience shame, disappointment, fear of judgment.
They try to "live as before," but suddenly realize it's no longer possible (irreversible consequences appear).
These should be specific, real-life situations frequently encountered among the target audience segment, without vague formulations. The more precise, the better.
What does an ordinary day look like for the client when the problem intensifies and becomes unbearable?
What events and factors suddenly make the problem the most important and urgent?
Why didn't they solve it earlier, and what held them back?
What was the last straw, after which they could no longer endure?
How did they search for a solution but didn't trust themselves, experts, products?
