📈 3. ANALYZING THE CURRENT LANDSCAPE
Stage 1: Identifying awareness levels
The classic Hunt Ladder has 5 levels:
Level 0: No problem (unaware of the need)
Level 1: Unaware problem (feeling discomfort)
Level 2: Seeking solutions (recognized the problem)
Level 3: Choosing a method (comparing approaches)
Level 4: Comparing products (choosing a provider)
Level 5: Ready to buy (making a decision)
Stage 2: Audience distribution
Based on the data, determine:
What percentage of the audience is at each level
Key characteristics of each group
Speed of transition between levels
Barriers and triggers for movement
Stage 3: Building the decision-making path
For each transition between levels, describe:
What insights the person needs to gain
What objections to overcome
What evidence to see
What emotions to experience

⚠️ IMPORTANT PRINCIPLES OF APPLICATION
1. Individuality of each product:
The Hunt Ladder is a tool that needs to be adapted to a specific product, audience, and market. There are no universal solutions.
2. Dynamic nature of the process:
The audience can move between levels in both directions. It is important to constantly track and adjust the strategy.
3. Data quality:
The effectiveness of the analysis directly depends on the quality of the source data. It is better to conduct fewer interviews but of higher quality.
4. Focus on results:
The goal is not to produce a beautiful analysis, but to achieve specific sales results.
5. Testing and adjustment:
The strategy must be constantly tested and adjusted based on real audience feedback.

📋 STRATEGY READINESS CHECKLIST
Before launching, make sure you have:
✅ Clear distribution of the audience by Hunt levels
✅ Understanding of the main barriers at each level
✅ List of key messages for broadcasting
✅ Content plan for the entire warm-up period
✅ Ways to measure effectiveness
✅ Response scenarios for main objections
✅ Backup strategies for low conversion
Result of applying the methodology: A systematic launch strategy that consistently leads the audience from complete unawareness to purchase through understanding their internal processes and targeted impact on key pain points.
