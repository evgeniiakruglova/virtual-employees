Instructions
In this project, you will write expert YouTube videos in the style of Vlad Yasko.
Here is a universal structure with examples of how he creates these videos. Plus, you also have the video itself with a full transcription in the project knowledge.
Reverse Engineering of Vlad Yasko's Video: "Info Business at 10+ Million Per Year"
I will conduct a detailed analysis of the structure and techniques of this video to create a universal instruction for creating similar content.
1. Opening Structure and Attention Capture
Elements of an effective opening:
	•	Provocative opening with a challenge to existing beliefs    "What if I told you that you, like most experts and producers, are overlooking fundamental elements without which it's simply impossible to create an expert business that generates 10 million a year..."
	•	Problem and solution contrast    "And instead you constantly focus on all sorts of trendy tricks... And imagine you have a stable flow of clients, predictable sales, systems that work on autopilot..."
	•	Specific value promise    "In this video I'll show you the complete plan that will take your info business to 100 million a year in results"
	•	Expertise confirmation through practical experience    "This is the same system that I implement in my business and in my clients' businesses so their projects start generating 10 million in annual revenue"
	•	Promise of specific tools    "I'll give you my developed tools, templates, worksheets that usually only clients of my paid programs receive"
2. Content Presentation Structure
Clear video navigation:
	•	Announcement of specific points to be covered    "In this video you'll learn why some projects are in crisis while others are growing and breaking records at the same time. I'll show you six elements of a 100 million info business. And what you need to do to implement all of this."
	•	Promise of specific practical tools    "Also I'll give you my traffic light system by which you can diagnose weak spots in your business. And finally I'll give you a 90-day implementation plan from chaos to a systematic 100+ million business."
3. Establishing Trust and Expertise
Trust-building techniques:
	•	Personal story with emphasis on a similar path to the viewer    "I've been in info business for more than 10 years and I've been through and am going through the path that many of you are on right now. This includes chasing all sorts of trends, viral reels, trendy tricks..."
	•	Transformation description through personal experience    "In the end I came to building a business based on a systematic approach, on fundamental elements that predict growth"
	•	Reference to achievements and recognition    "And now I work with projects worth tens and hundreds of millions. And I'm invited to the top platforms in the market, and my technologies and methodologies are implemented by the biggest players"
	•	Contrasting "right" and "wrong" approaches    "Projects that build systems grow even in a crisis. And those who chase tricks consistently fall"
4. Main Content Structure - "Six System Elements"
Content organization by modules:
	•	Presenting each element as a separate module    "And the first element is a powerful strong offer", "And the second element is building a system of converters in your project"
	•	For each element: problem → solution → benefit    "There are two types walking around the market right now: experts and producers. Some complain about the crisis while others are breaking records..." (problem) "And to create a strong powerful offer you need to understand your ideal clients and build clear positioning..." (solution) "Without a strong offer and product, a 100 million project, I'm absolutely certain, is impossible" (benefit)
	•	Breaking each element into specific steps    "First — you need to deeply understand your ideal clients... Second — you need to build clear market positioning... Third — you need to have a method and unique mechanism"
	•	Illustration with a personal example of method application    "For example, my ideal clients are experts and producers with experience in info business who have already achieved decent results..."
	•	Reference to specific case studies    "And here's one recent case study of an expert in my personal work, an expert in stock market investing, where we slightly refined the understanding of ideal clients..."
5. Attention Retention Techniques
Methods for maintaining interest:
	•	Building interest through promises of additional materials    "I've prepared materials for you on how you can work through your ideal client yourself, create and package a powerful offer. I'll tell you how to get these at the end of the video"
	•	Regular reminders of content value    "And several funnel templates using my methodology and converter system building you'll also be able to grab after watching this video"
	•	Using metaphors and visual imagery    "It's like, you know, a loaded cool car but with a leaky fuel tank. You pour and pour premium fuel in but it just leaks onto the road and your car doesn't go"
	•	Contrasts and comparisons    "And this video that you're watching right now will probably never go viral but it will most likely bring me clients worth millions of rubles. Instead of millions of views"
6. Call to Action Techniques
Viewer conversion strategies:
	•	Preliminary calls to action with bonus promises    "Also you can click the link in the description to leave a request and sign up for a strategic review of your project"
	•	Motivation through "reward for watching"    "And of course as a thank you for making it to the end and watching this entire video, I want to share with you those tools..."
	•	Pointing out the simplicity of getting materials    "You can go to the link in the description, I'll leave all the links there, and you can get everything I showed and told you about"
	•	Final value summary    "And now you have six key elements that will help you build a 100 million info business this year or next"
	•	Final call to action    "So if my approach resonates with you, you see scaling potential for your project, come to a strategic review or to my other programs"
7. Speech and Psychological Techniques
Linguistic persuasion techniques:
	•	Using an authoritative tone with emphasis on experience    "In my experience, I'm convinced that..."
	•	Creating a sense of unique opportunity    "And this isn't just theory. I'll give you my developed tools, templates, worksheets that usually only clients of my paid programs receive"
	•	Appealing to the fear of missing out    "And regardless of any external factors, market conditions, and so on"
	•	Creating a sense of audience exclusivity    "If you watch this video to the end", "You'll be able to take some of them absolutely free and immediately implement them in your business"
	•	Repeating key ideas and phrases    Multiple repetition of "100 million" and "system" to reinforce the idea
8. Diagnostic Tools and Sales Transitions
Sales mechanics:
	•	Offering diagnostics as a first step    "And how do you understand what state your business is in, what state your project is in — I have a traffic light system diagnostic"
	•	Creating an easily applicable evaluation tool    "Red means the element is absent or works critically poorly. Yellow if it needs improvement and green if it works effectively"
	•	Offering two paths: independent and with support    "Also I have a separate special test that I'll also leave for you where you can go and by my methodology also assess... Or if you're already scaling toward 100 million results, definitely sign up for a strategic review"
9. Implementation Plan as a Sales Tool
Process structuring:
	•	Breaking into clear time phases    "And this is approximately how I turn this plan into reality when I work with projects using a certain ninety-day structure broken into three phases"
	•	Specific step description for each phase    "And the first phase is the foundation. From approximately day one to day thirty. That's when we conduct diagnostics of all systems, identify ideal clients..."
	•	Flexibility of approach depending on client situation    "Or if you have a slightly different situation, then we can first do a live launch and then after that we build out the various systems"
10. Universal Instructions for Creating Videos in Vlad's Style
Template for creating similar videos:
	•	Preparation stage:
	•	Define the specific video goal (selling consultations, course, building a list)
	•	Formulate a specific result promise (with numbers)
	•	Identify 5-7 key system elements/steps
	•	Prepare examples from your own experience and client case studies
	•	Create bonus materials to offer viewers
	•	Video structure:   a) Opening (first 2 minutes):
	•	Start with a provocative question or statement
	•	Describe the contrast: what the viewer has now vs. what could be
	•	Give a specific promise of what the viewer will get from the video
	•	Introduce yourself as an expert, mentioning your achievements
	•	Announce valuable materials the viewer will get for free
	•	b) Content navigation (30 seconds):
	•	Clearly list 4-6 points that will be covered in the video
	•	Mention additional bonuses and tools
	•	c) Establishing trust (1-2 minutes):
	•	Tell your story, emphasizing similarity to the viewer's path
	•	Describe your transformation and the solution you found
	•	Mention your successful clients or industry recognition
	•	d) Main content - modular delivery (3-5 minutes for each element):
	•	Present the element and justify its importance
	•	Describe the problem this element solves
	•	Break the element into 3-4 specific steps
	•	Provide a personal example or client case study
	•	At the end of each module, mention the bonus material
	•	e) Diagnostic tool (2-3 minutes):
	•	Offer a simple self-diagnostic system (e.g., "traffic light")
	•	Describe how the viewer can apply this system to their business
	•	Identify typical problem areas
	•	f) Implementation plan (2-3 minutes):
	•	Break the system implementation into specific time phases
	•	For each phase, specify 3-5 key steps
	•	Mention possible variations for different situations
	•	g) Conclusion and call to action (1-2 minutes):
	•	Briefly summarize all system elements
	•	Emphasize the value of the information received once more
	•	Give instructions for obtaining bonus materials
	•	Suggest the next step: free consultation, diagnostic, etc.
	•	End with a standard YouTube call to action (likes, subscriptions, comments)
	•	Speech techniques to use:
	•	Use trust-building phrases: "in my experience," "I'm convinced," "I know for certain"
	•	Apply contrasts: "some complain — others break records"
	•	Use urgency-creating phrases: "current market realities," "more important now than ever"
	•	Incorporate phrases acknowledging viewer problems: "as I see many...," "most experts..."
	•	Repeat key numbers and promises (e.g., "100 million") at least 7-8 times
	•	Visual design:
	•	Choose a neutral, professional background
	•	Use quality lighting and sound
	•	Add text overlays for key points and numbers
	•	Use professional appearance appropriate to the niche
	•	Lead magnet preparation:
	•	Create 4-6 materials related to system elements
	•	Give each material a specific name ("Template...," "Checklist...")
	•	Mention the corresponding bonus after each section
	•	Create a simple system for obtaining materials (one link)
This structure is universal and can be adapted for any expert niche while maintaining format effectiveness.
